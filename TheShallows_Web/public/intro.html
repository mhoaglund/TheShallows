The Shallows is an interactive technology system. It is designed to do four things:

  1. To collect, miniaturize, and represent a set of gestures, properties and phenomena (data collection, user agency, control, consent, ease and unease) common to technology at large.
  2. To mimic the ambiguous moment of acceptance which occurs upon the arrival of a new technology in your life.
  3. To bring to light the material weight of the weightless experience of screens.
  4. To stage a comparison between distinct types of technology-facilitated cultural labor.

This project is built around a set of found objects chosen by a group of contributors during an in-depth workshop. Contributors chose objects in response to time-limited prompts concerning scarcity, association through dependence, and collection-making. As a viewer, you are invited to rearrange these objects by digitally creating or manually enacting sets of instructions, called Change Orders. Change Orders can be created by any viewer using the tablet on the left podium, and are printed automatically and placed on the right podium. Change Orders can be retrieved and enacted by anyone who wishes to. Instruction sets that have already been performed are recorded and can be browsed on the glowing ledger on the other side of the installation.
